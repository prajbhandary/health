Take Control of Health 🌿

Lifestyle changes that can genuinely improve all four of Dad's conditions.

💡
The good news: with the right diet, activity, and habits, it's possible to reverse pre-diabetes, reduce prostate symptoms, and significantly lower blood pressure — all without additional medications.
🥗

Diet — Fat-Free + Diabetic Diet

Gallbladder: Strictly avoid oily, fried, and fatty foods (ghee, dalda, mutton fat, cream). Even a single fatty meal can trigger gallbladder inflammation again.

Blood Sugar: Eat smaller portions 4–5 times a day rather than 3 large meals. Reduce white rice, white bread, sweets, and sugary drinks. Add more vegetables, lentils, and whole grains. Avoid mango, banana, grapes in large amounts.

Prostate & Kidneys: Drink 8–10 glasses of water during the day (but reduce after 7 PM to minimize nighttime urination). Reduce caffeine and alcohol completely.

🚶

Gentle Daily Movement

A 20–30 minute morning walk daily is the single most effective thing for blood pressure, blood sugar, and cholesterol together. Start with 10 minutes if needed and build up. Avoid strenuous heavy lifting or straining while the gallbladder and kidneys are still healing. No bed rest — gentle movement is encouraged.

🌙

Better Sleep (Especially with Prostate Issues)

Nighttime urination is a prostate symptom and will improve as Dutas shrinks the prostate over weeks. In the meantime: reduce fluids after 7 PM, avoid caffeine after noon, sleep with head slightly elevated to help nasal congestion. A saline nasal rinse (like Nasoclear or Jal Neti) can help the nasal congestion from Urader.

🧂

Reduce Salt and Sodium

His sodium level was slightly low (133, normal 137–145) and blood pressure was elevated. Avoiding excess salt (especially pickles, processed foods, packaged noodles) helps control blood pressure. A low-sodium diet of under 5g of salt per day is recommended for hypertension.

📵

Avoid These Completely

• Alcohol — Damages the liver, raises blood sugar, worsens gallbladder inflammation
• Smoking — Worsens all cardiovascular and kidney conditions
• Fatty/Oily foods — Triggers gallbladder flare-ups
• High sugar foods — Pushes pre-diabetes toward full diabetes
• Dehydration — Increases kidney stone and infection risk

😌

Stress Management

Chronic stress raises blood pressure and blood sugar. Light meditation, breathing exercises (Pranayam for 10 minutes daily), listening to music, or spending time with family all help. The recovery period is a good time to allow rest and avoid unnecessary mental strain.
